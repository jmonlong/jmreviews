Cancer, chromatin loop and SVs

Chromatin loop anchor points (LAPs) as sites of regulatory importance but also genomic fragility. They harbour an excess of SV breakpoints in cancers, are prone to double strand breaks in somatic cells, and are bound by DNA repair complex proteins.

Kaiser & Semple bioRxiv 2017

TE suppression in cancer

TE suppression proposed as a mechanism for immune escape in acute myeloid leukaemia (AML) and myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS). TE are less expressed in high-risk vs low-risk, or in resistant cancers. Too much TE expressed would cause interferon signaling and cancer cell killing.

Colombo et al Sci. Reports 2017

dplyr tricks

Using enquo to enquote a variable and !! to unquote it. For example:

starwars_mean <- function(var) {
  var <- enquo(var)
  starwars %>%
    group_by(!!var) %>%
    summarise(mean_height = mean(height, na.rm = TRUE),
            mean_mass = mean(mass, na.rm = TRUE),
            count = n())
}

starwars_mean(homeworld)
