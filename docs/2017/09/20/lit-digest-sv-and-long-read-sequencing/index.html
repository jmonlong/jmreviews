Mapping And Phasing Of Structural Variation In Patient Genomes Using Nanopore Sequencing

Stancu, bioRxiv 2017

  • 2 patients with congenital abnormalities, with MinION (11x/16x).
  • Better at finding complex chromothripsis.
  • 3K novel SVs, majority being duplication <200 bp.
  • Phasing showed the de novo chromothripsis occurred in paternal chromosomes.
  • Helps a lot for complex SVs.

Discovery and genotyping of structural variation from long-read haploid genome sequence data

Huddleston, Genome Research 2016

  • 2 haploid genomes.
  • Long-read is much more sensitive.
  • Highest gain for “small” SVs: 7bp - 1 Kbp.
  • 90% SVs are novel.
  • 60% can be genotyped with short reads once alternate allele is included.
  • Genotyped in 30 TGP samples. Some SVs look fixed.
  • Haploid is very useful for detection.

Resolving the complexity of the human genome using single-molecule sequencing

Chaisson, Nature 2015

  • Resolve inversions, complex insertions and long tandem repeats.
  • Enrichment in long short STR regions and complex insertions (?).
  • Strong enrichment of STRs (15x) among closed gaps. 78% of the gaps were covered by STR more than 10%.
  • 85% of the CNVs were novel, especially for insertions (92%).
  • 2.8x enrichment of STR insertions compared to deletions. STR insertions were 2.9x longer.
  • STR insertions clustered at the last 5 Mbp of the chromosomes.
  • Thousands of STR insertions within genes, 2 in exons.
  • Other complex insertions enriched in regions already enriched in MEI.
  • L1 and SVA insertions are found adjacent to repeats, more than in 1KGP or short read catalogs.
  • 68% of inversions flanked by inverted repeats.

Assembly and diploid architecture of an individual human genome via single-molecule technologies

Pendleton, Nature Methods 2015

  • Identified complex SVs
  • Phased SNVs and SVs
  • Identified large tandem repeats and suggest that TR are larger than in the reference genome.
  • Observed DNA transduction when MEI.
  • Complex STR-like insertion within STRs.
  • 4.2% of SVs are complex.
  • Inversions are enriched for complexity (55%).
